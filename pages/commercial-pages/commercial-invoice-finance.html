Debtor Finance transforms your outstanding invoices into instant cash flow by using your accounts receivable ledger as security. Unlike a traditional loan, this facility provides an advance based on the money your customers owe you, accelerating cash flow and funding growth without increasing debt.

Finco Capital partners with specialist lenders offering two main types of debtor finance to suit your business needs:

  • Invoice Factoring: The lender takes over management of your sales ledger and collections—streamlining your administration.
  • Invoice Discounting: You retain confidential control of collections, maintaining full ownership of your customer relationships.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why use debtor finance?
It bridges the gap between invoicing and payment, providing immediate cash to cover operational expenses, staff salaries, new projects, or growth initiatives—without waiting 30–90 days for payment.
Is debtor finance suitable for my business?
Ideal for B2B or B2G companies experiencing growth but constrained by slow payment cycles. Popular in wholesale, manufacturing, staffing, and professional services.
Do I need to provide property security?
Primarily secured against your accounts receivable, debtor finance generally does not require traditional property collateral—though lenders may request a general security agreement over business assets.
What is the difference between factoring and discounting?
Factoring: The lender manages your ledger and collections, saving administrative time but notifying customers of the arrangement.
Discounting: You maintain control and confidentiality over collections; your customers are unaware of the finance facility.

Common Questions

How do Reverse Mortgages work and am I eligible?
You borrow against your home's equity, with loan and accrued interest repaid when you sell, move into aged care, or pass away. Eligibility usually requires being 60 or older and owning an Australian property.
What can I use the funds for?
Funds are unrestricted—common uses include supplementing retirement income, home repairs, medical expenses, lifestyle enhancement, or supporting family.
How much can I borrow?
Loan amounts depend on your age, property value, and lender criteria. Older homeowners and higher property values generally allow larger loans.
Will it affect my Age Pension?
The loan itself is not assessed for pension eligibility, but how funds are used may impact means tests. Consultation with a financial advisor is strongly recommended.
Can I still live in my home?
Absolutely. You maintain full ownership and residency rights, provided you uphold property upkeep, council rates, and insurance.

Important Regulatory Protections (2024-2025)

Australian law mandates a No Negative Equity Guarantee—you will never owe more than your home's market value upon sale. Lenders must provide detailed projections of equity impact over time and ensure independent legal advice before loan finalisation. Joint borrowing safeguards spouses and long-term partners, protecting your home until the last borrower vacates.
